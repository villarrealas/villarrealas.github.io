Career Profile

I am a PhD Candidate working at the University of Pittsburgh with research interests in dark matter halo modeling and assembly bias, galaxy formation, and weak lensing statistics. The projects that I work on attempt to span the divides between theory, simulation, and observation in the hopes of being able to gain more information than one would be able to gain from any individual methodology. An example of this is the use of simulation results to inform methods of reducing systematic biases in joint probe analysis of surveys such as the Large Scale Synoptic Telescope (LSST).

These research interests have lead to a lot of programming work in which I have developed proficiency with Fortran, C, and Python. I have also used statistics environments such as Matlab and R, machine learning in Python packages such as scikit-learn, and determined model constraints using methodologies such as Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) using the package emcee. I hope to continue to learn new methodologies and techniques in order to deal with the increasing robust, high-dimensional datasets that we can expect to become the future of astrophysics within the next decade.
